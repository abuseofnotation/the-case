===

This day the wheather at London was unusually clear and Ayer could see the whole yard of the building up to the metal fence which was surrounding it. When Vince returned, Kei was already out and was walking down the alley that led to the main entrance. At first she started slowly, and then she stopped for a moment like she had noticed something and headed to another direction straight through the garden. Ayer leaned through the window to see the cab which was waiting for her and memorize the brand and the plate number.

"So?" Ayer heard the voice of Vince who had already returned to his chair, "What do you think about this girl?"

"She is cute." Ayer said.

"Cute?"

"Yes, I like her. But I *do* think she is lying. Listen, I have to go now, catch you later, OK?"

And before Vince had a chance to react, he got out of the room and ran to the exit, while simultaneously dialing in the number of the taxi which that Kei used:

"Hallo," he said with the best approximation of a Japanese accent "I want to order a taxi, but my phone is dead... Yes, my name is Cheng Lee..."

It took a second before the boy responded. Ayer used it to make one huge jump and finish off the stairs on his way to the exit.

"The address? Oh, Jee, it was in my phone... No, I don't have a profile. But you know, my wife has one. Can you check the address from her? She's Kei Lee."

He was already running towards the street as the support agent was waiting for Kei's profile to load.

"Yeah, thanks, what is her last trip's destination?"

Ayer closed the phone as soon as he got it and then ordered a cab from another company.

He arrived just in time to see her entering in an old, but well-maintained building at the city center (Ayer immediately crossed out "debt" as a possible reason for Den Lee's disapearance from Japan). He rushed at the other end of the street both to escape from her field of sight and to be able to catch a glimpse of the windows of the staircase as he was passing by and easily traced the apartment where she entered. He knew that that he had to do from this moment on was to wait a little, hoping that his intuition was right, and that she would leave her apartment at the evening.

---

He was sitting in a small Cafe. A few papercups of coffee were rolling on his table, he was holding his phone in front of his face. On its screen, there was a football match he couldn't care less about - placed here to provide him with an oportunity to concentrate on the door of the Kei's apartment, which was just at the other side of the street. 

For some time nothing happened but then he saw her. The next time the waitress came to his table he was gone. Along with the papercups, on his table laid a few crushed banknotes. Ayer rejoiced. He was in nearly perfect shape, and these old buildings with all of their ornaments and bumps were perfect for clmbing. The only thing that could stop him was if someone saw him, but that was next to impossible - people had long stopped paying attention at what was happening around them. That was what the surveilance system is for, right?

In thirty seconds time he was already in. He started going through Kei's personal belongings which were spread throughout the apartment. There were a lot of cosmetics, books written in English and Japanese and also a lot of high-end jevelery which was laying on the desk. A notebook containing text and sketches grabbed Ayer's attention, but going through it proved to be quite useless - even after doing so, in his mind she was even more faceless than she was at the beginning. Nothing that she said or did left a clue about her identity. She was not generic, as some other people for whom there wasn't much to know about to begin with, just faceless - a shadow with a pretty silhoette.

Still, in her night-stand he found, as he expected, the letters that she had exchanged with her sister.

He took the topmost one. Judging by the address, this one was written by Den Lee, but he could guess this by the envelope alone, and by the paint stains that it was covered in. Took another one, which was presumably the response to the first one - it was written by Kei with a very refined handwriting and had such a perfect alignment that he almost wished it wasn't so perfect, looking for the invisible, (or perhaps the unexisting) traces of pencil-drawn straight lines while examining it. But he couldn't find them. And after a while the doorlock snapped, the door hit the rubber stopper and the motion sensor bleeped - Kei was back.

Ayer had just a second to make a decision. So he slipped Den's letter in his pocket, rushed through the window, jumped on the sill and disappeared.

Ayer was standing on his desk and the only thing on it were the five sheets of paper, which were inside the envelope  he stole from Kei's room. He was inspecting them very carefully, but he was not happy. In fact this was one of the rare moments when he wished he thought more before acting. Maybe it was the excitement or maybe it was just wishful thinking, but somehow he failed to realize that if a Japanese person conversed with another Japanese, they had no reason whatsoever to use a foreign language. Translating a letter from Japanese to English was not a hard task by itself, but it was next to impossible in the situation Ayer was in - he didn't think that his vague suspicions could justify breaching  privacy, so he couldn't just hand it to *anyone*, and the people whom he was working with weren't too fond of "off the table" operations. For a first time, Ayer realized how dependent he had become of the people and resources which he used in the last several months. At the end he decided that he would contact the Scotland Yard and hope for the best. 

"Listen Vince, don't ask me how but I think I aquired the letters that Den Lee sent to her sister before she died. The problem is that-"

"Cadman report to the Scotland Yard headquaters immediately."

"Why what happened?"

"I think you can deduce it! Kei had filed a complaint for breaking and entering. The Detective had scanned the files and guess what it saw. The penalty is one month in the jail for minor offences."

"OK, can we settle that when all this is over."

"There is no 'all this', Cadman." Vince was doing his best not to show any emotions. "And there is no way to postpone your penalty - once a conviction has been issued, then neither I nor anyone else can modify it." Vince made a long pause as if he had something on his mind but wasn't sure whether he should say it. "You have 24 hours," he said instead.

Ayer did not know what he could do or whether he had a choice at all, but the one thing certain was that he had to talk to his colleagues at the Yard. The following morning he went to visit them. He prepared his luggage in a backpack in case they took him away and went to check with his landlord Mrs Johnson and tell her what happened: "Listen, I might dissapear for some time. I don't want to be a burden, if I am not back I will be OK if you can throw away the most part of my stuff and get a new tenant."

"Nevermind your stuff." she replied, "Where the heck are you going?"

"Let's say that I started a job which I am not sure how would I finish," he smiled.

"Ah, as unsertain about himself as always," she reached out and touched his cheek, "in your job as well as your love life."

"I have no reason to be sertain about myself," Ayer said.

"Have I told you enough times that critics are the most unhappy people on Earth?" she asked and then became serious: "Can I help you, Ayer?"

"If you happen to know someone who speaks Japanese." Ayer said.

"But I do," she said. "*I* do."

Ayer recognized Kei right away, although she had turned her back on him. Her looks were again flawless and, as the last time. She sat alone in a crowded bar without paying much attention to her surroundings. He sat next to her and touched her shoulder. She turned to him and stared at him with her eyes just a few sentimeters away from his. Some people had such eyes that staring at them feelt like he was staring directly into their soul - Kei's eyes left in him the impression that he is looking into a dark and endless void.

"What do you want, Mr Cadman?" She asked.

"I wanted to give you this," he took the letter from the pocket of his coat and put in in the table.

"You don't want your colleagues to find it in your possesion?" she asked.

"No, I want to apologise."

"You broke into my apartment." she said without touching the letter, "you went through my belongings and stole some of them. And now you return it and you expect me to be nice to you?"

"Negative. I don't expect that from you," Ayer turned to her and tried to smile, "Just take it," Kei slowly reached out for it and slid it to an area of the table that was close to her. "Now, if you would be willing to forget my behaviour from our last meeting, I want to ask you something: How did it all happened?"

"I want to ask you something too," Kei said, "Who gives you the right to get your nose in my personal life? And I mean that literary - your colleagues told me that what you did was illegal."

"Breaking and entering is illegal in every country that has laws in the first place." Ayer smiled.

"And why do you keep bothering me?"

"OK, the reason is that I was mistaken when it comes to you. And this does not happen to me very often, actually rarely. And for this reason I am very keen to learn your story."

"Your curiosity is your problem," she continued, "Thank you for returning this, but-"

"There isn't any reason to hide it you know?" Ayer continued. 

"I didn't hide anything. I told her that she-"

"Yes and you were marvelous in your ability to deceive. And also very experienced, if I may say so. You even deceived me, before I could figured it out."

"Because you are such a capable investigator?"

"Because I am in a very similar position in some respects." Ayer said. "That is the truth."

"What?" In spite of her makeup and her constant mannerisms, at that moment Kei looked even younger than she actually was. "What are you talking about? What do you want from me?"

"I just want to know. No, scratch that - I want *you* to tell me?"

"What was this bullshit about you being in similar position?"

"Why aren't your parents with you?"

"I don't know, why did they throw her out of our house? And did not allow me to speak to her? How can you even think that-"While she was speaking, Ayer was mesmerised by her face, seeing it changing, becoming more youthful, more expressive and more characteristic of her as emotions were making it turn red. "Alright, you know, I will tell you the *whole* damn thing. Just promise that you would listen and walk away. So firstly, I have no idea how you could think that my sister would involve herself in some petty crimes. Secondly..."

She made a long pause and continued speaking in a very different tone.
